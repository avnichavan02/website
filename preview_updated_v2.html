The Basics

What is Science Olympiad?

Overview

Science Olympiad is a national STEM competition for middle and high school students. Teams of 15 members compete in 23 events spanning biology, chemistry, physics, earth science, engineering, and more — all in a single tournament day.

23 Events

Events are split across science study tests, lab practicals, and hands-on engineering builds — there's something for every kind of scientist.

15 Members per Team

Each school fields one team of up to 15 students, with members specializing in different events and collaborating throughout the year.

Invitational Tournaments

Colleges and high schools host invitationals throughout the year for practice before official regional, state, and national competitions.

Competition

Science Olympiad Events

Science Olympiad features 23 events each season across three categories: Science & Study, Lab & Engineering, and Trial/Build events. Events rotate yearly.

Science & Study Events
Science
Anatomy & Physiology
Identify structures and answer questions on the human body systems designated for that year.
Science
Astronomy
Answer questions on stellar evolution, galaxies, and the designated deep-sky object topics.
Science
Cell Biology
Cover organelles, cellular processes, microscopy, and the designated organisms each season.
Science
Chemistry Lab
Perform chemistry lab tasks including titrations, unknown identification, and quantitative analysis.
Science
Disease Detectives
Apply epidemiology principles to analyze outbreaks and determine causes of disease.
Science
Dynamic Planet
Study Earth's systems — this year's topic rotates among oceans, glaciers, tectonics, and climate.
Science
Ecology
Analyze ecosystems, biomes, food webs, and environmental science concepts.
Science
Entomology
Identify insects and answer questions on insect biology, taxonomy, and ecology.
Science
Fermi Questions
Estimate answers to unusual quantitative questions using order-of-magnitude reasoning.
Science
Forensics
Analyze evidence samples and use chemistry and biology techniques to solve a crime scenario.
Science
Fossils
Identify fossil specimens and answer questions on paleontology, stratigraphy, and evolution.
Science
Geologic Mapping
Interpret topographic and geologic maps, cross-sections, and earth structure questions.
Lab & Inquiry Events
Lab
Experimental Design
Design, conduct, and report a controlled experiment using provided materials in a set time.
Lab
Measurement & Data
Take precise measurements, analyze data sets, and apply statistics to scientific questions.
Lab
Microbe Mission
Use microscopy and lab techniques to identify microorganisms and answer microbiology questions.
Lab
Optics
Build and use optical devices; answer questions on reflection, refraction, and ray diagrams.
Lab
Wind Power
Construct a wind turbine blade system that maximizes electrical output in timed test runs.
Engineering & Build Events
Build
Bungee Drop
Design an elastic cord system to drop an egg as close to a target height as possible without breaking it.
Build
Electric Vehicle
Build a battery-powered vehicle to travel a precise distance and stop as close to a target as possible.
Build
Flight
Design a rubber-band-powered aircraft for maximum time aloft within weight and size constraints.
Build
Roller Coaster
Engineer a track and car system to complete a course in a target time with the lowest deviation.
Build
Scrambler
Construct a device that uses a falling mass to propel an egg as close to a wall as possible without cracking it.
Build
Tower
Build a lightweight wooden tower to hold as much load as possible relative to its own mass.
